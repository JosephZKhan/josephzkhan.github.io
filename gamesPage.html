I was lucky enough to work with Ground Shatter for 2 years on Knights In Tight Spaces, the follow-up to BAFTA-nominated roguelike deckbuilder Fights In Tight Spaces. I was the most junior of a programming team of 3, in an overall team of 15.

I lead the implementation of the procedurally generated world map using Voronoi cells, and has a large hand in adapting and expanding the gameplay framework to allow support new actions for the many abilities and status effects in the turn-based combat.

Another notable ongoing task was setting up the definition assets for game elements, including abilities, cards, levels and quests. This involved extensive usage of Unity's editor scripting capabilities to lay out class properties in an intuitive way for designers to make adjustments.

I also set up several of the game's UI screens, giving functionality to designs provided by others, and in the process became familiar with handling both mouse/keyboard and gamepad input for menu navigation between a range of UI elements and layouts.

This internship was an unforgettable experience with a wonderful team of talent, which I credit with pulling back much of the curtain of mystique surrounding the nature of professional games development.

The project was made in Unity, with Inkle for narrative scripting. Plastic SCM was used for source control. Slack and Clickup were used as management software.

Available now on Steam for PC! Free demo also available.

I have spent the past year working on Enfrosted, a 3D collectathon platformer in a whimsical snowy setting with power-ups aiding in navigation and exploration. This is a group project for my final year of university. I took on the roles of Lead Design, Programming, Writing and Management in a team of 9 people.

A version of the game is due for completion by May as the assessment comes to an end, but the team plans to continue development for an eventual PC release on Steam later this year.

It is made in Unreal Engine 5, using FMOD for additional audio operations. Git has been used for source control. Trello, Github Wiki and Google Sheets are used as management software.

Game Jams

I have also participated in several game jams since 2019. I love the spontaneous creativity game jams can inspire! I have worked solo and with groups, in Unity, Unreal and Godot.

Featured: Enter Planet Bubbulon

Enter Planet Bubbulon: A fast-paced 2D arcade game I developed solo in Unity for the 2025 Global Game Jam, where the given theme was "Bubbles". Brainstorming unique ways to use bubbles lead to the idea of mining a bubble-planet, which naturally came with a form of danger in bursting the very planet you stand on.

I created the pixel art assets in Aseprite. I limited myself to the ZX Spectrum colour palette, going for a style similar to Jetpac.
